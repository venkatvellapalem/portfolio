# Finally Built My Portfolio - Now What?

There’s something oddly satisfying about typing your own domain into a browser and seeing *your* work staring back at you.

Not tutorials.  
Not someone else’s repo.  
Not “clone this project in 10 minutes” nonsense.

Mine.

---

## The Idea

For a long time, I kept telling myself:

> “I’ll build my portfolio when I’m good enough.”

Classic mistake.

Because “good enough” is a moving target.  
And honestly, if I waited any longer, I’d probably end up building a portfolio explaining why I *never built one*.

So I just did it.

---

## The Build

The goal was simple:

- Minimal  
- Fast  
- No unnecessary noise  
- Feels like *me*

And yeah - I overthought everything:
- spacing
- colors
- fonts
- even the tiny arrow icons

Because obviously, that’s what matters at 2 AM.

---

## The Structure

I wanted a **system**:

- **Projects** → things I've built  
- **CTFs** → my learning arc  
- **Blogs** → thoughts + writeups  
- **Whoami** → context, not a boring bio  

Each section answers a different question:
- *What can I build?*
- *How do I learn?*
- *What do I think?*
- *Who am I?*

That’s the idea.

---

## The Reality Check

Not gonna lie - it wasn’t smooth.

There were moments where:
- nothing looked right
- design felt off
- features broke randomly
- and motivation just dipped

And the worst part?

Opening your own site and thinking:

> “This is… mid.”

Yeah. That hits.

---

## But Here’s the Thing

Even with all that -

This is the first time everything I’ve been doing:
- learning
- building
- breaking things
- fixing things

…actually lives in one place.

And that changes something.

---

## Why This Blog Exists

I don’t want this to be a “perfect portfolio”.

I want it to be:
- a **log of my journey**
- a **record of my thinking**
- a place where I document everything I learn

Because forgetting what you learned is worse than not learning at all.

---

## What’s Next

More projects.  
More CTFs.  
More mistakes (definitely).  
More blogs.

This is just the starting point.
